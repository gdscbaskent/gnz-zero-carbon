What is Carbon Footprint?

Carbon footprint is a metric that measures the amount of greenhouse gases that an individual, an organization or a country releases directly or indirectly into the atmosphere over a certain period of time. This metric is usually expressed in units of carbon dioxide (CO2) equivalent and is usually measured in kilograms (kg CO2e).

The concept of carbon footprint is used to assess environmental impact at a personal, corporate or national level and to direct sustainability efforts. This monitoring is important to identify sources of greenhouse gas emissions, develop mitigation strategies, and evaluate measures taken to reduce environmental impact. The basic components that make up the carbon footprint are:

Direct Emissions: Direct emissions that are under the control of a person, organization or country arise from activities such as fuel consumption, industrial activities and energy production.

Indirect Emissions: These emissions are emissions from sources used for the production of goods and services, transportation and energy production. That is, it includes emissions that occur during the production of products or services purchased by the consumer.

Import and Export Emissions: Trade between countries includes emissions generated during the production of a country's goods and services. Emissions during the production of imported products are included in the carbon footprint of the importing country.

Carbon footprint calculation evaluates the environmental impact of an organization or individual by taking these factors into account. This assessment provides a basis for developing strategies to enhance environmental sustainability efforts, increase energy efficiency, and reduce greenhouse gas emissions. In addition, monitoring carbon footprint also contributes to the creation of effective policies in the fight against global climate change.
